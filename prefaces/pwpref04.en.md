# Abbreviations of Works, 2

[page V]

**Gold.** = Goldstücker's Wörterbuch.

**Gop. Br.** = Gopathabrâhmana in the Bibl. ind. (Roth).

**Govindân.** = Govindânanda, glossator on Çaṁkarâḱârja's Comm. on Bâdar. (Kern).

**H.** = Hemaḱandra's Abhidhânaḱintâmani, ed. by Böhtlingk and Rieu.

**Halâj.** = Halâjudha's Wörterbuch, ed. by Aufrecht.

**H. an.** = Hemaḱandra's Anekârthasaṁgraha.

**Hâr.** = Hârâvalî.

**Harisv.** = Harisvâmin.

**Hariv.** = Harivaṁça. With one number the older Calc. edition is meant, with three numbers the more recent lithographed one.

**Harshaḱ.** = Harshaḱarita, Calcutta 1876 (Kern).

**Hâsy.** = Hâsjârnava, Bombay 1757 and Ms. (Cappeller).

**Haug, Acc.** = Haug, Ueber das Wesen und den Werth des vedischen Accents. München 1874.

**Hemâdri** = Hemâdri's Ḱaturvargaḱintâmani in the Bibl. ind.

**Hem. Yog.** = Hemaḱandra's Jogaçâstra in Z. d. d. m. G. 28. From a manuscript Comm. Windisch most obligingly sent me many corrections and explanations.

**Hem. Pr. Gr. ed. Bomb.** = Hemaḱandra's Prâkrit-Grammatik, Bombay (Stenzler).

**Hiḍ.** = Hiḍimbavadha, ed. by Bopp.

**Hit.** = Hitopadeça, ed. by Schlegel and Lassen.

**Hit. ed. Johns.** = Hitopadeça, ed. by Johnson, London and Hertford 1847.

**Jâǵñ.** = Jâǵñavalkja's Gesetzbuch, ed. by Stenzler.

**J. A. O. S.** = Journal of the American Oriental Society.

**Ind. Antiq.** = Indian Antiquary (Roth).

**Ind. St.** = Indische Studien von Weber.

**Yogas.** = Jogasûtra.

**Yogat. Up.** = Jogatattvopanishad in the Bibl. ind. (Geldner and Roth).

**Jolly, Schuld.** = Jolly, Ueber das indische Schuldrecht, in Münchener philos.-philol. Abh. 1877, p. 287. ff.

**J. R. A. S.** = Journal of the Royal Asiatic Society (Kern).

**Kâç.** = Kâçikâ Vṛtti in the journal The Pandit VII. ff.

**Kâd.** = Kâdambarî, Calcutta Saṁvat 1919 (Kern).

**Ḱakr.** = Ḱakradatta, commentator on Ḱaraka and Suçruta, Ms. (Roth).

**Kâlaḱ.** = Kâlaḱakra, Ms. (Schiefner).

**Kâm. Nîtis.** = Kâmandakîjanîtisâra in the Bibl. ind.

**Kan.** = Kanâda's Vaiçeshikadarçana in the Bibl. ind.

**Kâṇḍ.** = Kâṇḍûpâkujâna in LA.

**Ḱaṇḍak.** = Kshemiçvara's Ḱaṇḍakauçika (Pischel).

**Kap.** = Kapila's Sâṁkhjapravaḱana. Windisch provided a complete index.

**Kâr.** = Kârikâ.

**Ḱaraka** = Ḱarakasaṁhitâ, Calcutta 1929 and 1877 and Mss. in the possession of Roth (Kern and Roth).

**Karmapr.** = Karmapradîpa (Stenzler). Appears under the wrong title Kâtjâjanasmṛti in the Dharmaçâstrasaṁgraha, Vol. I, p. 603—644.

**Kâṭh.** = Kâṭhaka (Weber).

**Kathâs.** = Kathâsaritsâgara, ed. by Brockhaus.

**Kâty. Śr.** = Kâtjâjana's Çrautasûtra, according to Weber's Index.

**Kâty. Dh.** = Kâtjâjana's Dharmaçâstra (Stenzler).

**Kâty. Snânas.** = Kâtjâjana's Snânasûtra (Stenzler).

**Kauś.** = Kauçika's Sûtra to the AV., Mss. (Roth and Weber).

**Caurap.** = Ḱaurapaṅḱâçikâ, ed. by Boulen.

**Kaush. Âr.** = Kaushîtakâranjaka (Weber).

**Kaush. Up.** = Kaushîtakibrâhmanopanishad in the Bibl. ind.

**Kautuḱar.** = Kautukaratnâkara, Ms. (Cappeller).

**Kâvyapr.** = Kâvjaprakâça, Calcutta 1866. Cited by Ullâsa and Sûtra, unless S. (page) and Z. (line) are added.

**Kenop.** = Kenopanishad in the Bibl. ind.

**Chandom.** = Ḱhandomanǵarî, edited by Brockhaus in Berichte über die Verhandlungen der Kön. Sächsischen Gesellschaft der Wissenschaften zu Leipzig. Phil.-hist. Kl. Bd. VI, 1854.

**Chând. Up.** = Ḱhândogjopanishad in the Bibl. ind.

**Kir.** = Kirâtârǵunîja, Calcutta 1814.

**Kṛshis.** = Parâçara's Kṛshisaṁgraha, Calcutta 1862 (Roth).

**Kshitîç.** = Kshitiçavaṁçâvalîḱarita, ed. by Pertsch.

**Kshurikop.** = Kshurikopanishad in the Bibl. ind.

**Kull.** = Kullûka, commentator on the Manu.

**Kumâras.** = Kumârasaṁbhava, ed. by Stenzler.

**Kumârasv.** = Kumârasvâmin, commentator on the Pratâpar. (Pischel).

**Kusum.** = Kusumânǵali, ed. by Cowell.

**Kuvalay.** = Kuvalajânanda, according to two lithographed editions.

**LA.** = Lassen's Anthologie 3rd ed.

**Laghuk.** = Laghukaumudî, ed. by Ballantyne, Benares 1867 (Cappeller).

**Lalit.** = Lalitavistarapurâna in the Bibl. ind. (Kern).

**Lâṭy.** = Lâṭjâjana's Çrautasûtra in the Bibl. ind.

**Lîlâv.** = Bhâskara's Lîlâvatî, Calcutta 1846 (Kern).

**L. K.** = Laghuk.

**Lot. de la b. l.** = Burnouf, Lotus de la bonne loi.

**M.** = Mânavadharmaçâstra.

**Madanav.** = Madanapâla's Madanavinoda, Benares 1869 and Ms. (Roth).

**Mâdh. Kâlan.** = Mâdhava's Kâlanirnaja (Weber).

**Mahâbh.** = Mahâbhâshja, lithographed in Benares.

**Mahâbh. (K.)** = Kielhorn's edition of the Mahâbhâshja.

**Mahâvîrac.** = Mahâvîraḱarita, ed. by Trithen.

**Mahîdh.** = Mahîdhara, commentator on the VS.

**Maitryup.** = Maitrjupanishad in the Bibl. ind. The accents, which on account of their faultiness were often omitted or marked as suspect by (!), Schröder took from a Ms.

**Maitr. S.** = Maitrâjanî Saṁhitâ, being prepared for publication by L. Schröder. Since the accented Mss. were not at hand right at the beginning, some accents could only be supplied in the addenda (Schröder).

**Mâlatîm.** = Mâlatîmâdhava, according to two editions, Calcutta 1830 and Bombay 1876.

**Mâlav.** = Mâlavikâgnimitra, ed. by Tullberg.

**Mâṇḍ. Çikshâ** = Mâṇḍûkî Çikshâ (Weber).

**Mân. Gṛhy.** = Mânavagṛhjasûtra, according to a Ms. in the University Library at Bombay (Schröder).

**Mân. K. S.** = Mânavakalpasûtra, according to citations in Gold.

**Mantrabr.** = Mantrabrâhmana, Calcutta 1872 (Roth).

**Mârk. P.** = Mârkandejapurâna in the Bibl. ind.

**Mat. med.** = The Materia medica of the Hindus compiled from Sanskr. medical works by Udoy Chand Dutt Civil Medicinal Officer. With a Glossary of Indian Plants by Ge. King Superint. R. Bot. Garden Calc. and the author. Calcutta 1877.

**Mayr, Ind. Erb.** = Das indische Erbrecht von Aurel Mayr, Wien 1873.

**MBh.** = Mahâbhârata, cited by Parvan, Adhjâja and Çloka of the Bomb. edition. The older Calcutta edition with two numbers is adduced only when it offers a divergent reading.

**Med.** = Medinîkoça.

**Med. avy.** = Avjajânekârthavarga at the end of the Medinîkoça.

**Megh.** = Meghadûta, ed. by Stenzler.

**Mit.** = Mitâksharâ. With two numbers the Vjavahârâdhjâja, Calcutta 1829, is meant; with two numbers followed by a or b together with indication of the line — the complete work in 4to.

**M. K. S.** = Mân. K. S.

**Mṛcch.** = Mṛḱḱhakaṭika, ed. by Stenzler. Comm. to Mṛḱḱh. are the extracts in the notes to my translation of this drama.

**Mudrâr.** = Mudrârâkshasa, according to two editions, Calcutta 1831 and 1926 (Cappeller).

**Nâgân.** = Nâgânanda, according to two Calcutta editions (Cappeller).

**Naish.** = Naishadhaḱarita.

**Nâr.** = Nârada's Dharmaçâstra (Jolly).

**Nigh. Pr.** = Nighantuprakâça (Roth).

**Nîl.** = A rational Refutation of the Hindu Phisosophical Systems, by Nehemiah Nîlakantha S'âstrî Gore. Translated etc. by Fitz-Edward Hall. Calcutta 1862.

**Nîlak. mit einer Zahl** = Nîl.

**Nîlak.** = Nîlakaṇṭha, commentator on the MBh.

**Nîlar. Up.** = Nîlarudropanishad in the Bibl. ind. (Geldner and Roth).

**Nir.** = Nirukta, ed. by Roth (Roth).

**Njâm.** = Ǵaiminîjanjâjamâlâvistara, ed. by Goldstücker and Cowell (Kern).

**Njâs.** = Gotama's Njâjadarçana in the Bibl. ind. The edition Calcutta 1828 is designated by the addition of (1828). (Kern).

**N. K.** = Njâjakoça or Dictionary of the technical Terms of the Njâja Philosophy, by Bhîmâḱârja Jhalakîkar. Bombay 1875.
